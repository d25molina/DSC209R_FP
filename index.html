Inspecting U.S. Voter Turnout

a project by Matt Zidell, Jacob Sease, and Dimas Molina

Image source: https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2022/10/drives-american-voters

The Pew Research Center reported that the two most recent U.S. presidential elections were among the highest-turnout elections in the past century, with about 66% turnout in 2020 and 64% in 2024. But those national numbers hide the real story: turnout varies by state and shifts in surprising ways over time. Also, turnout rates swing largely between presidential election years vs state level elections. We wanted to understand these trends, and what long-term patterns emerge when we look beyond a single election. These questions motivated our project. Write-up

In this prototype, we decided to present the VEP and VEP turnout rate statistics for each U.S state by using a Mapbox map and displaying these values through an interactive tooltip. We also analyzed the statistics from various election years between 1980 and 2022 using a slider that highlights every midterm and presidential election year. In addition, we provided readers the option to not just analyze the statistics for a specific year, but also to analyze them over the years covered in our data. By clicking on a state, readers can see a line chart of how VEP and VEP turnout rate varied over the years.

The most challenging part of our project will be designing visuals that maintain the right balance between clarity and depth. Although we are only focusing on two core statistics, they still create many possible angles for analysis and therefore, it is important we do not overwhelm readers with too much information at once. Our goal is to build an interactive storytelling experience that is simple, intuitive, and consistent, requiring careful decisions about how each element is styled and how the data is presented. Ultimately, our challenge lies in making the interface feel effortless for readers, even though the design work behind it is complex.

Key Takeaway

Voting eligible population (VEP) and VEP turnout rate do not move together.
